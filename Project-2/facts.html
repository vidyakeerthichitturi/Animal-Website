FUN FACTS

Did you hear about the hummingbird that has a beak longer than its body? Or the koala that can spend up 22 hours of the day asleep? Or how about the fact Komodo dragons can have virgin births?

  • There's a frog that can fly. Yes really! The Wallace's flying frog - one of the weirdest frogs in the world.
  • Flamingos are pink on the inside, not just the outside.
  • Octopuses dies after giving birth.
  • Snails have teeth, lots of them - around 14,000 to be precise. The teeth are set in a jaw called a radula.
  • Cuttlefish can change colour in 200 milliseconds, as fast as a human can blink.
  • An elephant's trunk contains tens of thousands of individual muscles (many more than in the entire human body)- and is quite obviously the biggest nose in the world.
  • Male seahorses give birth. Seahorses display a kind of reversed pregnancy – after fertilisation, eggs are transferred into the male’s brood pouch to develop.
  • Swans are renowned for forming intense, long-term, and often lifelong monogamous pair bonds, acting as symbols of love and fidelity.
  • Cheetahs are the fastest animals in the world, reaching speeds of up to 120kph or 75mph.
  • There are animals that can live forever - well, sort-of. Immortal jellyfish, along with at least five other jellyfish species
  • Humans aren't the only animal to enjoy 'highs'. Dolphins use toxic pufferfish to ‘get high’.
